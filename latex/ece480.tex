\sectiona{Abstract}
  
  This project presents the development and validation of a modular, low-cost load and direction-sensing system designed for Formula SAE vehicle testing. The system provides an adaptable alternative to expensive commercial sensors by integrating strian gauge-based load sensing with uniaxial and triaxial accelerometers to capture dynamic force magnitude and direction.

  \vspace{-0.2cm}

  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Dynamic Response:} Captured synchronized transient strain and 3-axis acceleration. Peak acceleration values reached \textbf{41.5g (Y-axis)} and \textbf{56.0g (Z-axis)}, with clear oscillatory decay.
    \item \textbf{Signal Processing:} The \textbf{Kalman Filter} significantly enhanced data quality, reducing noise standard deviation by \textbf{17.0\% (Y-axis)} and \textbf{18.5\% (Z-axis)} while maintaining a high correlation \textbf{($>$0.95)} with raw physical events.
    \item \textbf{Vibration Characterization:} Frequency-domain analysis (FFT) identified dominant structural vibration modes between \textbf{15–35 Hz}, matching the expected natural frequencies of the test apparatus.
    \item \textbf{System Performance:} Achieved high-fidelity data acquisition with sampling rates exceeding \textbf{100 kS/s}, ensuring no loss of transient data during impact events.
  \end{itemize}
